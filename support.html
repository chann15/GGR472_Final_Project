Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

WHY IS THIS IMPORTANT?

Student housing in Canada is in crisis, with only 1 in 10 university students able to access residence beds. As a result, 1.2 million students rent off-campus, driving up rental prices for everyone (Lamoureux, 2024).

In Toronto, the issue is even more pronounced. Despite 90,000 students enrolled in 2023, the University of Toronto provides just over 2,000 on-campus beds (Langer, 2024). This housing shortage forces students into expensive off-campus rentals (Jeffords, 2024).

The Need for Better Housing Solutions
  • High Demand, Limited Supply: Between 25% to 63% of Canadian students live in unsuitable housing (Statistics Canada, 2021).
  • “Vertical Studentification”: Many students are pushed into high-rise apartments rather than traditional shared housing.
  • Newcomer Challenges: International and out-of-city students often struggle to navigate Toronto’s rental market (Mu & Soong, 2025, p. 4).
  • Rise in Rental Scams: Fraudulent listings on platforms like Airbnb and Facebook Marketplace are a growing concern (Blosser, 2023; Cocola-gant & Calvo, 2023, p. 431).
HOW OUR WEB MAP HELPS

Unlike general rental platforms like Rentals.ca and Zillow, our web map is designed specifically for students. While UofT provides in-person housing services, it lacks an interactive rental map that combines:

  • Student-specific filters to match housing needs.
  • Web map features that enhance spatial awareness and location-based search.
  • A safer, verified platform to help students find reliable rentals without scams or misinformation.

Our solution bridges the gap between student needs and accessible housing, offering a streamlined, user-friendly way to find accommodations with confidence.

FEATURES

Our web map offers several key capabilities to enhance your navigation and daily planning:

  • Extensive Connectivity Filter – Select a location via the search bar or clickable TTC station icons, then choose your travel mode and maximum travel time. This provides a realistic estimate of your commute, especially to campus and faculty buildings.
  • Grocery & Park Locator – Toggle grocery stores and parks within your travel range to see how location affects daily errands and leisure activities.
  • Time-Saving Insights – Understand how different locations impact the efficiency of routine tasks, helping you optimize your time.
  • University Resources – The navigation bar includes a dropdown with essential University of Toronto websites, offering easy access to key student services and support.
